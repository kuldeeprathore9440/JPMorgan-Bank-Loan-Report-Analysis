This Bank Loan Analysis project presents a comprehensive and structured approach to understanding lending performance, portfolio health, and borrower behavior using interactive dashboards.
The primary objective of the project is to transform raw loan data into meaningful insights that can support strategic decision-making in a financial institution.
Through the use of advanced data visualization techniques, the dashboard highlights key performance indicators such as total loan applications, total funded amount,
total amount received, average interest rate, and average debt-to-income ratio (DTI). These metrics are further enhanced with Month-to-Date (MTD) and Month-over-Month (MoM) comparisons, 
enabling stakeholders to track growth trends and performance fluctuations over time. By integrating these KPIs into a single unified view, the project ensures that users can quickly
assess the overall health and efficiency of the bank’s lending operations without diving into raw data.

A major strength of this project lies in its ability to distinguish between good and bad loans, which is critical for evaluating credit risk and financial stability.
Good loans, defined as those that are fully paid or currently active, make up a significant majority of the portfolio, contributing positively to the bank’s revenue through consistent repayments.
In contrast, bad loans, categorized as charged-off loans, represent a smaller portion of the total portfolio but have a disproportionately large impact on financial losses.
The dashboard effectively visualizes this contrast by presenting both percentage distribution and absolute values, allowing users to understand not only the scale but also the severity of risk exposure.
Additionally, the loan status grid provides a detailed breakdown of performance metrics across different loan categories, including funded amounts, received amounts, interest rates, and DTI levels. This
layered analysis enables deeper insights into how different loan segments behave and where risk mitigation strategies should be focused.

The Overview dashboard extends the analysis by exploring trends and patterns across multiple dimensions, offering a more granular understanding of borrower behavior and loan distribution.
Monthly trend analysis reveals a steady increase in loan applications, indicating consistent demand and growth in lending activities. Regional mapping adds a geographical perspective,
helping identify areas with higher lending volumes or potential risk concentrations. Further segmentation by loan term shows a clear preference for longer-term loans, which, while beneficial
for generating higher interest income, also introduce increased risk due to longer repayment periods. Employment length analysis highlights that individuals with longer work experience are more
likely to secure loans, suggesting that employment stability plays a crucial role in creditworthiness. Similarly, loan purpose analysis uncovers that debt consolidation is the most common reason
for borrowing, which may signal underlying financial stress among customers. Home ownership patterns further enrich this analysis by showing how different living situations correlate with borrowing behavior,
offering valuable insights for targeted lending strategies.

The Details dashboard complements the broader analysis by providing a transaction-level view of loan data, allowing users to drill down into individual records and examine borrower profiles in depth.
This level of detail is essential for identifying micro-level patterns that may not be visible in aggregated views. For example, relationships between loan grade, interest rate, and repayment
performance can be analyzed to understand how risk-based pricing impacts outcomes. Similarly, patterns in installment amounts and DTI ratios can help identify customers who may be at higher risk of default
due to financial strain. Overall, the project successfully combines high-level strategic insights with detailed operational data, creating a powerful analytical tool for financial decision-making. The findings
suggest that while the bank is experiencing strong growth and profitability, there is a need to carefully manage risk, particularly in segments associated with high DTI, long-term loans, and debt consolidation
purposes. By leveraging these insights, financial institutions can enhance their lending strategies, minimize losses, and build a more resilient and sustainable loan portfolio.
